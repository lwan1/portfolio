The Hummingbird

DuPont, Washington

Hummingbirds possess remarkable spatial memory, retaining knowledge of feeder placements for years. They also adeptly monitor flowering peaks and recall previously visited flowers. This ability is attributed to the substantial size of the hippocampus in a hummingbird's brain, which is specialized for learning and spatial memory.
